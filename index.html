01

Business question

How accurately is the Nordic & Baltic demand plan predicting actual shipments across categories and markets, and where should the Area Demand Planner intervene to protect product availability while avoiding excess inventory?
02

Data preparation

  • ✓ Built a fictional but realistic transactional dataset: 28 SKUs × 5 markets × 52 weeks, approximately 6,000 rows.
  • ✓ Modelled seasonality, campaign weeks, new product launches, lead times, stock availability and OOS events.
  • ✓ Cleansed history conceptually by flagging OOS-distorted weeks so they do not pollute the statistical baseline.
  • ✓ Joined SKU master with weekly shipment, forecast and customer-order data.
03

KPI design

KPI Definition Why it matters for Barilla Nordic
Forecast Accuracy 1 - |Actual - Forecast| / Actual Anchor KPI for the Area Demand Planner role.
Forecast Bias (Forecast - Actual) / Actual Detects systematic over/under-forecast per market and category.
MAPE Mean absolute % error Comparable error across SKUs of different sizes.
Service Level Shipped / Customer Orders Direct link to product availability and customer satisfaction.
OOS Risk Share of SKU-weeks with stock < 90% Early warning for supply and replenishment issues.
Campaign Uplift Promo vs. baseline volume lift Calibrates commercial input into the forecast.
04

Forecast analysis

↗ Baseline weeks

Pasta and Sauces in Denmark and Norway are predicted within tight error bands; statistical baseline is doing its job.

↗ Promotional weeks

Systematic under-forecast: planner uplifts trail observed sell-out; service level drops and OOS risk peaks.

↗ Launches

First 8–12 weeks of new SKUs are over-forecast in some markets and under-forecast in others; no consensus loop with NPD/Sales.

↗ Crispbread

Q4 seasonality captured; Finland and Sweden are the volume engine.

↗ Baltics

Small volumes, high coefficient of variation; SKU-level statistical forecast is too noisy.

05

Stakeholder implications

Sales & Key Account

Calibration of promo plans and listings; commercial input drives uplift assumptions.

Trade Marketing

Post-promo evaluations feed back into a reusable uplift library by category and market.

Supply Chain / Plants

Earlier visibility on volume swings stabilises capacity, lead times and safety stock.

Finance & S&OP

Consensus forecast aligns volume, value and margin; reduces both lost sales and obsolescence.

06

Recommended actions

  • → Weekly Nordic demand review with Sales, Trade Marketing and Supply — one consensus number.
  • → Quarterly refresh of a promo uplift library per category × market, built from post-promo evaluations.
  • → Lock launch forecasts 8 weeks pre-go-live with NPD, Marketing and Key Account inputs.
  • → Move Baltics to category-level forecast with top-down SKU split; review safety stock for long-lead-time SKUs.
  • → Publish a one-page Nordic demand plan monthly and track Forecast Accuracy, Bias, Service Level and OOS Risk by market and category.
07

Process I would own as Area Demand Planner

Collect commercial input from Nordic Sales → cleanse history and rebuild statistical baseline → overlay campaign, launch and seasonality uplifts → run S&OP consensus → publish demand plan → coordinate with Supply Chain on capacity and inventory → measure, learn and feed back into the next cycle.
